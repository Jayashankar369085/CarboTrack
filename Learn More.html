Climate Change Basics

Climate change is a significant and lasting change in the statistical distribution of weather patterns over periods ranging from decades to millions of years. It may be a change in average weather conditions, or in the distribution of weather around the average conditions (i.e., more or fewer extreme weather events).

Source: National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)

The Importance of Reforestation

Reforestation is the process of restoring and replanting areas of forest that have been lost or damaged due to human activity, such as deforestation, logging, or wildfires. It is an important strategy for combating climate change, preserving biodiversity, and protecting ecosystems.

Source: National Geographic

Renewable Energy Explained

Renewable energy is energy that is generated from natural resources that are continually replenished, such as sunlight, wind, rain, tides, waves, and geothermal heat. It is considered a clean and sustainable source of energy, as it produces little to no greenhouse gas emissions or air pollution.

Source: TED-Ed

Ocean Conservation

Ocean conservation is the protection and preservation of ecosystems and biodiversity in the ocean and coastal areas. It involves efforts to reduce pollution, conserve marine habitats, and promote sustainable fishing practices to ensure the health and longevity of marine ecosystems.

Source: National Geographic
