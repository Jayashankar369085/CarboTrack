These days, you might be thinking about how to reduce your carbon footprint. It is impossible to miss news about the environmental effects of burning fossil fuels. While the problem is big, little changes you make every day can help contribute to a cleaner, greener future.

Before we talk about ways to reduce your carbon footprint, we must first ask, “what is a carbon footprint?” A carbon footprint is the sum of all the byproducts that come from using fossil fuels to manufacture and move goods, build structures for homes and businesses, provide food, and travel.

The gasses produced by these activities, namely carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide, are called greenhouse gasses because they trap heat in the atmosphere, raising the average temperature of the earth.

Reducing your carbon footprint means changing how you approach and participate in activities that produce greenhouse gasses. This guide will show you how.


Reduce Your Carbon Footprint Through Transportation

  • Drive less: Organize shopping trips to get more done on each outing, walk or bike when distances are shorter, and use public transportation as much as possible.
  • Go easy on the acceleration and brakes: Acceleration burns more fuel, so maintaining a smooth, even speed is more fuel-efficient.
  • Regularly service your car and keep tires properly inflated: Clean oil and belts, and properly inflated tires help your car run efficiently and use less energy.
  • Carpool: Sharing a ride can reduce your carbon footprint significantly.
  • Use cruise control: It helps maintain an even speed and improves fuel efficiency.
  • Cut down on air conditioning: Reduce usage of the most power-intensive appliance in your car.
  • Consider purchasing a hybrid or electric vehicle: They help eliminate the greenhouse gasses that conventional cars produce.
  • Avoid flying if possible: Air travel produces a significant amount of greenhouse gas.
  • If you must, fly nonstop: Taking off and landing burns more jet fuel than cruising at altitude.

Reduce Your Carbon Footprint Through Food

  • Eat less meat and stick with fruits, veggies, grains, and beans: Raising animals requires more energy than growing plants.
  • Choose organic and local foods that are in season: Reduces energy used for transportation and storage.
  • Reduce your food waste: Buying more food than you need and throwing it away wastes resources.
  • Compost: Reduces waste heading to landfills and creates useful organic matter for fertilizing your garden.
  • Use reusable cups, plates, utensils, bottles, and containers: Reduces the carbon footprint associated with disposable items.

Reduce Your Carbon Footprint at Home

  • Turn down your water heater to 120˚F: Most people don’t notice the difference when taking a shower or bath.
  • Lower your thermostat in winter and raise it in summer: Accepting a wider range of indoor temperatures can reduce energy usage.
  • Turn off lights and unplug appliances when not in use: Many appliances draw power even in stand-by mode.
  • Change incandescent light bulbs: Switching to LED or CFL bulbs eliminates waste and reduces landfill contributions.
  • Use a low-flow showerhead: Reduces water and energy usage.
  • Look for an ENERGY STAR® symbol when buying new products: ENERGY STAR® appliances are more efficient.
  • Choose renewable energy through Energy Choice: Select plans that power your home with green energy sources.
  • Do an energy audit of your house: Identify how to make your home more efficient.
  • Recycle effectively: Reduces waste going to landfills and gives plastics a second life.
  • Insulate your home: Reduces energy needed for heating and cooling.
  • Donate old clothes: Gives them a second life and maximizes the energy used to make and ship them.

Reduce Your Carbon Footprint When Shopping

  • Buy only what you need: Avoid wasteful purchases.
  • Bring a reusable bag: Eliminates waste from plastic bags.
  • Invest in quality products that last: Reduces energy used in manufacturing and waste for landfills.
  • Don’t buy fast fashion: Uses more water and energy for manufacturing and shipping.
  • Buy vintage or recycled clothing: Helps give items a second life and reduces demand for new products.
  • Support and buy from companies that are environmentally responsible and sustainable: Encourages sustainable practices.

Reduce Your Carbon Footprint by Taking Action

  • Purchase carbon offsets: Fund ways to reduce carbon emissions.
  • Talk to family and friends about climate change issues and carbon footprints: Sharing tips can magnify the impact.
  • Find local climate action groups: Share ideas and form groups to raise awareness.
  • Speak to your local representatives and vote on policies that protect the environment: Influence regulation and encourage adopting protective policies.
